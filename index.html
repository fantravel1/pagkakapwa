Pagkakapwa

The Global Filipino Identity Engine

Exploring kapwa (shared identity/interconnectedness), the core concept of Sikolohiyang Pilipino (Filipino Psychology).

What is Kapwa?

"Kapwa is the recognition that the self and others share an inner identity." — Virgilio Enriquez, Father of Filipino Psychology

Kapwa means "shared identity" — the recognition that the self (ako) and others (iba-sa-akin) share an inner being. This differs fundamentally from Western individualistic psychology, as kapwa implies an intrinsic connection rather than separation between self and other.

Academic Foundations

Virgilio Enriquez: Father of Filipino Psychology

(1942-1994)

Virgilio Gaspar Enriquez established kapwa as the foundational concept of Filipino social psychology during his pioneering work at the University of the Philippines. After earning his Ph.D. in Social Psychology from Northwestern University in 1971, he returned to the Philippines and founded the Philippine Psychology Research and Training House (PPRTH), launching a movement that would fundamentally reshape how Filipinos understand their own psychology.

The Kapwa Framework

Ibang-Tao (Outsider)

Five levels of interaction:

  • Pakikitungo — Civility
  • Pakikisalamuha — Mixing
  • Pakikilahok — Joining
  • Pakikibagay — Conformity
  • Pakikisama — Getting along

Hindi-Ibang-Tao (One-of-us)

Three deeper levels:

  • Pakikipagpalagayang-loob — Mutual trust
  • Pakikisangkot — Involvement
  • Pakikiisa — Being one with (highest level)

Related Values Structure

Core Value: Kapwa — shared identity
Pivotal Interpersonal: Pakikiramdam — shared inner perception
Linking Socio-Personal: Kagandahang-loob — shared humanity
Surface Values: Hiya (propriety), Utang na loob (gratitude), Pakikisama (companionship), Bahala na (determination)
Societal Values: Karangalan (dignity), Katarungan (justice), Kalayaan (freedom)
